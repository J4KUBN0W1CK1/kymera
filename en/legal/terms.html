4. Order and Formation of Contract

Interest in a work may be expressed via:

  • the contact form at kymera-art.com
  • e-mail to atelier@kymera-art.com
  • by telephone or in person by prior arrangement

Expressing an interest in a work does not constitute a binding order. A purchase agreement for a work is formed only when both parties have agreed upon:

  1. the specific work (or the brief for a commissioned work)
  2. the total price, including the method of delivery
  3. the delivery timeline
  4. the advance payment and payment terms

Confirmation of the agreement is sent by e-mail, and the artist will typically confirm receipt of the order within 3 working days.

5. Payment Terms and Advance Payment

Upon formation of the agreement, an advance payment of 30–50% of the total price of the work is due, generally within 7 days of the agreement being formed. The precise amount of the advance payment depends on the nature of the work and is agreed individually.

The remaining portion of the purchase price (the balance) is due prior to dispatch of the work, at the latest on the day of handover. Payment of the balance confirms the collector's readiness to receive the work.

Payment is made by bank transfer to the artist's account. Cash payment up to the statutory limit is also accepted upon personal collection.

Note: The advance payment (non-refundable deposit) serves, among other purposes, to cover the cost of materials and initial production. Should the buyer cancel the order after production has commenced, the advance payment or a portion thereof may be non-refundable — see Section 10 (Withdrawal from Contract).

6. Production Lead Time and Delivery

Each work is produced with full attention and care befitting its character. The standard production lead time is 4 to 12 weeks from receipt of the advance payment, depending on:

  • the complexity of the mechanism and finish
  • the dimensions and weight of the work
  • the current order schedule at the atelier

The precise timeline is always stated in the order confirmation. The artist keeps the collector informed of production progress and any changes to the timeline without undue delay.

7. Delivery and Handover

Czech Republic: The work is delivered personally by the artist in his own vehicle. Delivery within the Czech Republic is included in the price of the work. The artist will arrange the specific date and location of handover with the collector on an individual basis. Personal handover also allows for care and maintenance guidance to be given on-site.

International: For delivery outside the Czech Republic, the artist arranges transport via a specialist fine art or courier shipping service. International shipping costs, insurance, and any applicable customs charges are subject to individual agreement and are not included in the price of the work.

The work is packed prior to dispatch in a manner appropriate to its value and fragility. The buyer bears the risk of damage to the work from the moment of its receipt.

8. Authenticity and Certificate

Each KYMERA sculpture is accompanied by:

  • A unique identification number discreetly placed on the work
  • A certificate of authenticity signed by the artist, with a description of the work, the date of creation, and the piece number
  • Photographic documentation capturing the final state of the work prior to dispatch

The certificate is an integral part of the delivery. Loss of the certificate does not invalidate ownership of the work, but complicates future verification of authenticity. In the event of loss, the artist may issue a duplicate certificate upon request.

10. Withdrawal from Contract

Where the buyer is a consumer and the contract was concluded at a distance (without the physical presence of both parties), the buyer has the right to withdraw from the contract without stating a reason within 14 days of receiving the work (Section 1829 of the Czech Civil Code).

Important exception: The right of withdrawal within the 14-day period does not apply to works produced to order in accordance with the consumer's specifications, or works clearly adapted to a specific individual (Section 1837(d) of the Czech Civil Code). This exception applies to the majority of KYMERA commissions — whether the exception applies will always be stated in the order confirmation.

Withdrawal from the contract by the buyer — outside statutory rights — is subject to the following terms:

  • Before production commences: the artist will return the advance payment in full within 14 days.
  • After production has commenced, but before completion: the artist may offset demonstrably incurred costs. The remaining portion of the advance payment will be returned.
  • After the work is completed: the advance payment is non-refundable. The parties may agree an alternative arrangement (e.g. resale of the work to a third party).

The artist reserves the right to withdraw from the contract in the event of unforeseen loss or destruction of materials, incapacity, or other force majeure circumstances. In such cases, the full advance payment will be returned.

Return of payments: Any monetary amount due shall be returned by bank transfer to the buyer's account within 14 days of the claim being acknowledged, unless the parties agree otherwise (Section 1832 of the Czech Civil Code).

11. Complaints and Warranty

The artist warrants that the work:

  • corresponds to the description and documentation set out in the order
  • has been executed with professional care and quality materials
  • has mechanical parts that are functional at the time of handover
  • exhibits no hidden defects of which the artist was or ought to have been aware

A statutory warranty period of 24 months from the date of receipt by the consumer applies (Section 2165(1) of the Czech Civil Code (Act No. 89/2012 Coll.)). For business buyers, rights relating to defects are governed by Section 2099 et seq. of the Czech Civil Code, without a statutory warranty period; any quality guarantee is subject to individual agreement. A complaint must be submitted by the collector in writing to atelier@kymera-art.com or to the artist's address, with a description of the defect and photographic documentation.

Complaints do not apply to:

  • natural changes in materials (wood may move in response to humidity and temperature)
  • damage caused by improper handling, modification, or failure to follow care instructions
  • aesthetic variations that are a natural characteristic of a handcrafted original

A complaint is deemed received at the moment the written notification with photographic documentation is delivered to the artist's e-mail or postal address. The artist will confirm receipt of the complaint without undue delay, and no later than 5 working days. The complaint will be resolved within 30 days of being submitted (Section 19(3) of Act No. 634/1992 Coll.). If the artist fails to resolve the complaint within this period, the consumer has the right to withdraw from the contract or to request a reasonable reduction in the purchase price.

Return of payments: Any monetary amount due shall be returned by bank transfer to the buyer's account within 14 days of the claim being acknowledged, unless the parties agree otherwise (Section 1832 of the Czech Civil Code).

Care instructions are included with every work. Following them contributes to the longevity of the work and is a condition of warranty validity for complaints relating to mechanical parts.
